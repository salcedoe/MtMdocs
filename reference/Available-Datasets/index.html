    Available Datasets

    Zenodo

    A community of shared data sets

    • Total Segmentator CT Datasets
    • Total Segmentator MRI Datasets

    Request an academic licencse for total segmentator here: TotalSegmentator License. This will allow you to run the Total Segmentator extension on MRI datasets.

    The Cancer Imaging Archive

    The TCIA has massive amount of imaging used from cancer patients. The data is organized by cancer type. The website has a great feature that lists all the datasets and gives you information on the location of the scans, the modality used, number of subjects, whether the data has metadata associated with it and when the dataset was last updated. There is even an advanced search option for you to quickly locate an anatomical site. As of 2020, the website also houses COVID datasets. Most of the datasets are in a large DICOM file format.

    New Mexico Descendant Database (NMDID)

    Our dataset includes whole body CT scans of over 15,000 New Mexicans who died between 2010-2017. Each individual is represented by approximately 10,000 images. Slice thickness is 1 mm with 0.5 mm overlap. Warning, some of these cadavers died violent deaths

    Morphosource

    3D data representing the world's natural history, cultural heritage, and scientific collections. More antropologically focused with more Surface data than volumetric data (scanned surfaces).

    National Cancer Institute

    Imaging Data Commons

    • 3D Slicer extension to support access to the content of NCI Imaging Data Commons

    Human Organ Atlas

    The Human Organ Atlas bridges cellular and whole organ scales with images of whole intact organs at 8-20 m resolution and region of interest zooms at 1 m.

    Dryad

    • 7 Tesla MRI of the ex vivo human brain at 100 micron resolution

    Kaggle

    A data science competition website Small selection of datasets from Kaggle:

    • RSNA Intracranial Hemorrhage Detection: 5 different types of Intercranial Hemorrhages
    • Structural MRI Dataset: T1,T2, Flair datasets from the Neurohacking in R coursera class
    • COVID-19 CT scans: 20 CT scans and expert segmentations of patients with COVID-19
    • Pulmonary Embolism in CT images
    • NIH DeepLesion Subset: 32,120 axial computed tomography (CT) slices from 10,594 CT scans (studies) of 4,427 unique patients. There are 13 lesions in each image with accompanying bounding boxes and size measurements, adding up to 32,735 lesions altogether.
    • OSIC Pulmonary Fibrosis Progression: Patient with varying severity of decline in lung function from pulmonary fibrosis with CT scan of their lungs. Lung function is assessed based on output from a spirometer, which measures the forced vital capacity (FVC), i.e. the volume of air exhaled.
    • Brain Tumor MRI: Flair images of brains with tumors
    • Alzheimers Dataset: Very Mildly Demented to Moderately Demented Brains from Alzheimers Patients

    OASIS

    Open Access Series of Imaging Studies (OASIS) that is aimed at making neuroimaging datasets freely available to the scientific community. By compiling and freely distributing this multi-modal dataset generated by the Knight ADRC and its affiliated studies, we hope to facilitate future discoveries in basic and clinical neuroscience.

    University of Utah Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute

    There are a variety of datasets available here, one of the more interesting ones is ad diffusion tensor MRI set of the heart. They also have small animal CT scans which is unique. There is also a magnetic resonance angiogram of an aneurysm. These files are stored as .zip files that are then a collection of .nrrd files.

    Stanford Center for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine & Imaging

    Collection of public medical datasets. This contains Brain CTs, Brain MRIs, Chest Xrays, Cardiac Ultrasound, Lower Extermity Radiographs, Knee MRIs, Musculoskeletal Xrays, AI Imaging, and Bone Age Xrays.

    Head CT Scans associated with 'Validated automatic brain extraction of head CT images'

    The data consists of 35 different subject's non-contrast CT scan, with 2 subjects having 2 scans but every other subject having one scan (in tarballs). One subject additionally has 2 scans, but from the same time point, but with different convolution kernels. Each scan has at least one reader's manual segmentation of the image to delineate the mask of the brain areas (including cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)). The data are from the Minimally-Invasive Surgery plus rt-PA for Intracerebral hemorrhage Evacuation (MISTIE) Phases II and III clinical trials the Clot Lysis: Evaluating Accelerated Resolution of Intraventricular Hemorrhage (CLEAR) Phase III clinical trial. Each tarball contains a series of NIfTI (Neuroimaging Informatics Technology Initiative) files. Each image has manual segmentation from 2 different readers which is a binary image delineating the brain and an automated estimate of the brain by the method proposed in the paper below.
